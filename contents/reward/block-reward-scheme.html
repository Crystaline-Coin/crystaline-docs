Block Reward: Scheme

Introduction

This page briefs the reward system implemented in Crystalline which is the reference for how much reward will be given to a miner, given he mines a block at a certain blockchain height.

A reward is given to each individual who successfully mines a block on the Crystalline network based on the block height assigned to that mined block. The Crystalline proposed reward system ensures the distribution of wealth over a wider range of miners, thus expanding the network decentralization and in the long run, security.

Scheme

Although most current PoW and PoS systems utilize a constant reward function for the calculation of each block’s reward (e.g. The Bitcoin’s constant reward model, where the reward gets divided by 2, given some 210,000 blocks mined on top of the main branch as shown in figure 1), our method is based on a Geometric Reward Policy which provides more of a fair reward policy [1] for a greater variety of miners with different stakes, as Crystalline uses PoS for mining delegation, giving all of them a chance to ultimately mine a block. This method is designed to provide an alternative to the classic way of funds distribution in such PoS systems, where the rich continuously get richer.

Fig 1. Bitcoin contrast reward function halves the block mining reward, given a constant number of 210,000 blocks be mined after the last halving [2]

Distribution

Following Bitcoin's reward system, the wealth distribution converges to a normal random variable with a very low variance as shown in figure 2 [1]. This system will provide rewards only to a limited number of miners, having hash generation rates comparable to the entire hashrate of the network. This even gets worse as you move to a PoS system, where the wealth distribution directly determines the next miner on the network, giving a higher stake almost always a better chance of mining the next block.

Fig 2. The wealth distribution system for a Constant Reward system following a PoW (the blue curve), a PoS (the dotted curve) systems; and a Geometric Reward system for a PoS‌ (the green bins) system [1].

As shown in figure 2, by replacing a Geometric Reward calculation function in place of a constant one, one can achieve way more distribution in terms of the network wealth generation over the long run, even if certain groups have larger amounts of funds at stake in the beginning.
